A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

15 October 1931 - 27 July 2015

Indian Aerospace Scientist

Former President of India

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He spent the four decades as Scientist at Defence Research and Development Organisation(DRDO) and Indian Space Research Organisation(ISRO). He also knows as "Missie Man of India" for his work. Kalam was elected as president with support odf Bharitya Janata Party. He also referred as "People's President."

Timeline of Kalam Sir's life:

  • 1931:Born at Rameswaran born to Jainulabdeen, a boat owner, and homemaker Ashiamma. He went to Ramanathapuram Schwartz Matriculation School and worked from an early age to supplement the family income.

  • 1954:Graduates in Physics from Saint Joseph's College,Tiruchirappali

  • 1980:Launched India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle

  • 1981:Honoured by Government of India with Padma Bhushan

  • 1990:Awarded by Padma Vibhushan

  • 1997:Recieved Bharat Ratna

  • 1999: Appointed as India's Chief Scientific Advisor

  • 1998:Pokhran-II Nuclear Test

  • 2002:Elected AS 11TH President

  • 2012: Launched ‘What Can I Give Movement’, a programme for the Indian youth aimed at defeating corruption

  • 2015:Passed away due to Cardiac arrest in Shillong,Meghalaya
